# COSMOS Encyclopedia — Competitive Landscape Analysis

*March 2026*

## Executive Summary

COSMOS occupies a genuinely unique position in online astronomy education: it is the only astronomy encyclopedia of substantial size (644 articles) that is written entirely by active research astronomers, pitched at a general audience, and freely accessible. Its closest competitors either lack expert authorship (Wikipedia), are paywalled (Britannica), are fragmented across dozens of sub-sites (NASA), or are focused on a different purpose (Astrobites = research digest, not reference).

The main competitive threats are Wikipedia on breadth/SEO, AI-generated encyclopedias on freshness/scale, and NASA on institutional authority. The main opportunities are interactive visualisations, structured data/API, and bridging definitions with current research — none of which any competitor does well.

## Detailed Competitor Profiles

### 1. COSMOS — The SAO Encyclopedia (Swinburne)

- **URL:** https://ivolabbe.github.io/cosmos/ (new) / https://astronomy.swin.edu.au/cosmos/ (legacy)
- **Articles:** 644
- **Depth:** High — articles average 300-1500 words with figures, equations, image captions
- **Authority:** Very High — written by research astronomers at Swinburne CAS
- **Audience:** General public seeking detailed astronomical information
- **Freshness:** Moderate — content stable, site recently modernised
- **Unique features:** Dense internal cross-linking (lexicon-term links), some 3D-capable articles, curated coherent voice throughout
- **Citations:** Universe Today, Astronomy Cast, APOD, timeanddate.com
- **Mobile:** Good (new GitHub Pages version, responsive CSS)
- **Contribution model:** Closed (Swinburne researchers only)
- **Strengths:** Authoritative, substantial depth, dense cross-linking, free, coherent editorial voice, strong reputation
- **Weaknesses:** Relatively small corpus (644 vs Wikipedia's 30,000+), no API/structured data, limited interactive content, update cadence unclear

### 2. Wikipedia Astronomy Portal

- **URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal:Astronomy
- **Articles:** ~30,000+ under WikiProject Astronomy
- **Depth:** Variable — Featured Articles are excellent; stubs can be thin
- **Authority:** Variable — no expert vetting requirement, crowd-sourced
- **Audience:** Everyone
- **Freshness:** Excellent — continuously updated, breaking discoveries within hours
- **Unique features:** Wikidata knowledge graph (100M+ topics), free API, CC BY-SA, 300+ languages, revision history
- **SEO:** Dominant — ranks #1 for virtually every astronomy query
- **Mobile:** Excellent (dedicated app + responsive web)
- **Contribution model:** Open (WikiProject Astronomy has ~322 participants)
- **Strengths:** Unmatched breadth, SEO dominance, structured data via Wikidata, constantly updated
- **Weaknesses:** Inconsistent quality, no single authorial voice, variable accuracy, not curated as coherent educational resource

### 3. NASA/JPL Resources (Multiple Sites)

- **Sites:** NASA Science Universe Glossary, Imagine the Universe, Space Place, Basics of Spaceflight
- **Scope:** 1,000s of pages across fragmented sub-sites
- **Depth:** Moderate per glossary entry, deeper in feature articles
- **Authority:** Very High (it's NASA)
- **Audience:** Varies by sub-site (Space Place: ages 5-13; Imagine: ages 14+; Science: general)
- **Freshness:** Mixed — NASA Science glossary is current; Imagine the Universe structurally updated August 2021
- **Unique features:** MicroObservatory (real robotic telescopes), APOD, massive image/video archives, educational lesson plans
- **SEO:** Strong (.gov authority) but fragmented navigation
- **Mobile:** Mixed (modern NASA Science site is responsive; legacy sites are not)
- **Contribution model:** Closed (NASA staff/contractors)
- **Strengths:** Institutional authority, rich multimedia, real observational data, educator resources
- **Weaknesses:** Extremely fragmented, no single coherent encyclopedia, many legacy designs, no unified search

### 4. Astrobites

- **URL:** https://astrobites.org
- **Articles:** ~4,000 covering ~3,000 research papers
- **Depth:** Moderate — each post summarises one arXiv paper in accessible language
- **Authority:** High (written by graduate students, partnered with AAS Nova)
- **Audience:** Undergraduate and early-graduate astronomy students
- **Freshness:** Excellent — daily posts tracking the latest arXiv papers
- **Mobile:** Good (WordPress, responsive)
- **Contribution model:** Semi-open (graduate students apply and are selected)
- **Strengths:** Uniquely current, accessible writing, strong community, AAS partnership
- **Weaknesses:** Not a reference encyclopedia — it's a journal digest. No structured index by topic. Hard to use for lookups.

### 5. HyperPhysics — Astronomy Section

- **URL:** http://hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu/hbase/astro/astcon.html
- **Articles:** Part of ~6,000-page physics resource
- **Depth:** Moderate — concise explanations with equations and diagrams
- **Authority:** High (Rod Nave, Georgia State University)
- **Audience:** Physics/astronomy undergraduates
- **Freshness:** Poor — created 1998, minimal updates since ~2016
- **Unique features:** Pioneered concept-map navigation (clickable bubble diagrams). ~2 million page views/day.
- **SEO:** Very strong for physics queries (200,000+ inbound links)
- **Mobile:** Poor — 1990s HTML, fixed-width, no responsive design
- **Contribution model:** Closed (single author, now retired)
- **Strengths:** Concept-map navigation genuinely innovative, dense accurate content, massive user base
- **Weaknesses:** Visually antiquated, mobile-hostile, uncertain future (elderly single maintainer), TLS issues

### 6. ESA/Hubble Word Bank

- **URL:** https://esahubble.org/wordbank/
- **Articles:** 41 terms
- **Depth:** Low-Moderate — accessible definitions with Hubble imagery
- **Authority:** High (ESA)
- **Audience:** Students, parents, educators, general public
- **Freshness:** Moderate — regularly expanded, invites suggestions
- **Unique features:** Beautiful grid layout with Hubble thumbnail images, PDF gallery
- **Mobile:** Good (responsive grid)
- **Contribution model:** Closed (ESA communications team)
- **Strengths:** Gorgeous visual presentation, authoritative imagery, clear accessible language
- **Weaknesses:** Extremely limited scope (41 terms), Hubble-centric, no deep concept linking

### 7. Encyclopaedia Britannica — Astronomy Section

- **URL:** https://www.britannica.com/browse/Astronomy
- **Articles:** ~500-800
- **Depth:** Moderate — professional writing, multi-level (elementary/high school/adult editions)
- **Authority:** High (professional editors, commissioned expert authors)
- **Audience:** General public, students at multiple levels
- **Freshness:** Good — articles show 2026 update dates
- **Unique features:** Multi-tier access, quizzes/games, image galleries, expert editors
- **SEO:** Strong but typically outranked by Wikipedia
- **Mobile:** Good (modern responsive design)
- **Contribution model:** Closed (professional editors)
- **Strengths:** Authoritative, professionally edited, regularly updated, multi-level content, long reputation
- **Weaknesses:** Paywall for full access, generalist (astronomy is one section of hundreds), not written by working astronomers

## Market Gap Analysis

| Gap | Who does it? | Opportunity for COSMOS |
|---|---|---|
| Interactive 3D visualisations tied to articles | Nobody well | **Very High** — prototype built |
| Navigable knowledge graph | HyperPhysics (1998 design) | **High** — data already exists in cross-links |
| Structured data API / JSON-LD | Wikipedia (Wikidata) only | **High** — low effort, big SEO win |
| Textbook definition + latest research linked | Nobody | **High** — bridge COSMOS + ADS |
| Multi-level reading depth in one page | Nobody | **Medium-High** |
| Offline-capable astronomy reference | Nobody | **Medium** — trivial for static site |
| Embeddable widgets for educators | Nobody | **Medium** |
| Deep multilingual content | IAU (glossary only) | **Medium** (high effort) |

## Summary Positioning Matrix

```
                    HIGH AUTHORITY
                         |
          Britannica     |    COSMOS ★
          (paywalled)    |    (free, expert-written)
                         |
    LOW DEPTH -----------+------------ HIGH DEPTH
                         |
          ESA/Hubble     |    Wikipedia
          (41 terms)     |    (variable quality)
                         |
                    LOW AUTHORITY
```

COSMOS sits in the high-authority, high-depth quadrant with the unique advantage of being free and expert-curated. The upgrade path focuses on adding interactivity, structured data, and research currency to cement this position.
